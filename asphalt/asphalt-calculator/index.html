The tonnage formula

The one asphalt-specific twist versus a concrete takeoff: the answer converts to weight, because plants batch and bill by the certified scale ticket. Everything upstream — measuring, unit conversion, waste — is the same discipline. If you only need the volume, the volume calculator skips the density step.

Mistakes that blow asphalt orders

Four ways tonnage estimates go wrong

1. Using loose thickness instead of compacted — a 25% overstatement. 2. Assuming concrete's density (150 lb/ft³ vs 145) when converting old tables. 3. Forgetting that overlays need a leveling course on rutted pavement, which adds 10–20% tonnage that never shows in plan math. 4. Ordering exact: hot mix can't wait for a second truck — it's workable for roughly 2–3 hours after loading, less in cold wind.

Verify with the delivery ticket

Every load comes with a scale ticket showing net tons. Track tickets against this estimate during the pour — if you're at 60% of area but 75% of tonnage, the crew is running thick and you'll be a truck short by the end.
