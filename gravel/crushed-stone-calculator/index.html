Loose vs compacted: what you're really ordering

This is the concept that separates clean estimates from short ones: a compacted 4-inch base consumes about 4.5 inches of delivered loose stone. The calculator reports both volumes so the takeoff, the delivery ticket, and the finished grade all reconcile. Densities by size live in the crushed stone weight guide and the density database.

Compaction rules that protect the estimate

Three rules, no exceptions

Compact in lifts of 4 in or less — a plate compactor only densifies the top few inches. Compact every lift before placing the next, or the base stays soft at depth and settles under load. And keep clean stone and crusher run in their lanes: clean stone under drains and slabs-on-grade, crusher run under anything that carries wheels.
